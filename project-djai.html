UX / Product Design

DJai

Your Favorite DJ, Now in Your Pocket

Role UX / Product Design
Year 2024
Tools Figma

Overview

Current music platforms fall short in delivering truly dynamic and personalized listening experiences. DJai reimagines how people interact with music — not just passively listening, but actively shaping it.

Problem

Three core friction points define the modern listening experience:

  • Hard to discover new music. Algorithmic recommendations surface the same artists repeatedly, leaving listeners stuck in a loop rather than genuinely exploring new sounds.
  • Updating playlists is a full-time job. Curating and maintaining playlists that match shifting moods, contexts, and events demands constant manual effort with no intelligent assistance.
  • No app utilizes true DJing. Existing platforms treat listeners as passive consumers. There is no tool that brings professional DJ controls — EQ, mixing, and live transitions — into an everyday mobile experience.

Process

Research began with identifying two distinct user archetypes to ground design decisions in real behavior and goals.

Persona °1 — Irene, 27, Boston. A Nurse Practitioner who listens to music to decompress after long shifts. She wants smart, effortless curation that matches her mood without requiring her to manage it manually.

Persona °2 — Marko, 23, Los Angeles. A professional DJ who needs a mobile tool that mirrors the control he has behind the decks — real EQ, live mixing, and the flexibility to perform anywhere.

User flows were mapped across three core surfaces: onboarding and mood detection, the Now Spinning playback view (Basic and Advanced modes), and the DJ controls panel with EQ knobs and performance pads.

Solution

There is a missed opportunity to empower users to not just listen to music, but interact with it depending on their mood, setting, or event. DJai bridges the gap between passive listening and active performance — offering a Basic mode for effortless AI-curated playback and an Advanced mode that puts professional DJ controls directly in the user's hands.

The result is a single app that serves both Irene and Marko: intelligent enough to handle curation automatically, and expressive enough for those who want full creative control.
